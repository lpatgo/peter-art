Teaching Philosophy

"Building a solid foundation of technical skills and theoretical concepts is balanced with helping each student find their own voice."

My goal as a teacher is to create an open environment where students can take risks and ask questions. In this space, self-reflection, conversation, and debate thrive with respect to multiple perspectives. I provide projects that promote critical thinking and productivity. In addition, I organize trips to museums, libraries, and visiting artist lectures, which refresh and inspire student learning.

Although it is difficult to divide practice and theory, it can help young artists articulate themselves. For practice, we learn the fundamental skills needed to work with materials and understand process. To facilitate conceptual thinking, I encourage group problem solving, tangential thinking, and games that challenge traditional notions of authorship. For theory, we read texts that offer a variety of lenses to understand art scientifically, psychologically, philosophically, and biographically. Finally, I present lectures that examine the cultural and historical context of art pieces. The goal is to provide students with technical and conceptual skills, building towards a self-awareness that will support them as artists.

I provide useful tools, practical information, educational experiences, and emotional support for young artists. Making art is a gift for others. I see teaching as a similar activity. My focus is to guide students on their journey towards forming their own art practice and engaging society.
